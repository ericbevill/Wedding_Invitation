How to use this tool

Your quick companion for crafting a beautiful invitation

Four steps, and you're done. Changes save automatically on this device as you work.

I
Fill in your details
Use the 5 editor tabs — Couple, Event, Design, RSVP & Gifts, Print. Type and watch the preview update.
II
Pick a look
In Design, choose a theme, light/dark mode, and a font. Add a wax seal if you like.
III
Set up RSVP
In RSVP & Gifts, pick Link, Email, or Phone. Add a registry link if you want one.
IV
Export & share
Use the export buttons at the bottom of the editor — HTML for digital, PNG/PDF for print pieces.

What you can create

Digital Invitation
Mobile-friendly webpage with countdown, schedule & RSVP.
Printable Invitation
Polished 5×7 PDF ready for print shops or home.

Good to know Any field left empty is automatically hidden from the invite. The editor runs locally in your browser, and your photos stay on your device unless you choose to upload the exported files somewhere yourself.

Tap or click a tab below for a deeper walkthrough of each section.

The Couple tab

This is where your story begins — names, roles, and short bios.

Names & roles

Enter each partner's name. The Role fields (Bride, Groom, Partner, or anything you like) sit underneath. Roles fit about 20 characters.

Short biographies

Two or three lines about each partner is plenty. Mention how you met, a shared love, or something that makes you smile. Empty fields are hidden automatically.

Godparents or special guests

Scroll down to honour parents, godparents, or anyone you'd like to acknowledge. You can change the label to match your ceremony.

The Event tab

Set your date, time format, location, and full schedule here.

Date & formatting

Pick your date from the calendar picker, then choose a display format — seven options from "20 September 2025" to "09/20/2025".

Time format

Choose 24-hour (14:30) or 12-hour (2:30 PM) depending on what's common for your guests.

Event schedule

Add up to 10 events — ceremony, cocktail hour, reception, brunch. Each event has a name, venue, time, optional icon, and map link. The first five appear on the printable Details Insert.

Map links Paste a Google Maps URL so guests can tap for directions. Search your venue in Maps, tap Share, copy the link.

The Design tab

Shape the look and feel — colours, fonts, and optional wax seal.

Colour theme

Twelve curated themes — Gold, Blush, Sage, Navy, Mauve, Earth, Botanical, Plum, Antique Rose, Slate, Terracotta, and French Blue. Click a swatch to preview instantly.

Light or dark mode

Light mode is classic and airy; dark mode is dramatic and modern.

Font & font colour

Choose the display font for names and headings. Override the text colour with the colour picker if you want something like deep burgundy.

Wax seal

Add a romantic opening — guests see a monogrammed seal first and tap to reveal the invite. You can customise the label below.

RSVP & Gifts

Three ways to collect replies, plus an optional gift registry.

Choose how guests reply

Link — Send guests to a Google Form or wedding website.
Email — Show your email for guests to write to directly.
Phone — Show your number for calls or texts.

RSVP deadline

Add a deadline date — it appears beneath your RSVP block, formatted to match your date style.

Gift registry

Add your registry URL here, then decide where it appears. Turn on Show on digital invite for the online version, and use the Print tab toggles if you also want it on a printed card.

Footer note & dress code The footer note (dress code, parking, notes) lives in the Event tab, not here — look for the "Footer Note" section near the bottom of Event settings.

Photos

Add a hero image, two portraits, one landscape photo, or a small swipeable gallery depending on the layout you choose.

How to upload

Click or drag a photo into any upload box in the Couple tab. Click again to replace it, or use the ✕ button to remove it.

Layouts

Choose between Two Portraits, One Landscape, Gallery Strip, or Background Only. The controls below the picker change to match the selected layout.

Hero opacity

The hero photo sits behind the opening names. Use the opacity slider — 30–50% usually keeps names readable.

Best sizes Hero: portrait or full-height images around 1200×1800px work best (the hero fills the screen vertically). Portraits: square or slightly tall. JPG and PNG both work.

Exporting & sharing

All export buttons are at the bottom of the editor.

Digital invitation

Export Invite HTML gives you one standalone .html file. You can share it in two ways: send the file itself to guests, or publish it online as a wedding website link.

Option 1 — Send the HTML file

Click Export Invite HTML, save the file, then attach that .html file to an email or message. This is the quickest option if you want to send the invitation as a file.

Option 2 — Share it as a website link

If you'd rather send a clickable link instead of a file, you can publish the exported invite online for free using Netlify. This gives you a simple wedding website link to share with guests.

1. Click Export Invite HTML and save the file.
2. Create a folder on your computer called something like wedding-invite.
3. Move the exported HTML file into that folder.
4. Go to Netlify Drop at app.netlify.com/drop.
5. Create a free Netlify account or log in.
6. Drag your wedding-invite folder into the upload area.
7. Netlify will publish your invitation and give you a website link.
8. Open the published invite, then copy the link from your browser's address bar and send it to your guests.

After the wedding

If you would like to remove your invitation from the internet later, you can do that from your Netlify account.

1. Log in to Netlify.
2. Open your invitation project.
3. Go to Project configuration.
4. Open General and scroll to the Danger zone.
5. Choose Disable project to take the invitation offline but keep it saved in your account.
6. Or choose Delete site if you want to remove it permanently.

Need help? If you need any help exporting, publishing, taking your invitation offline, or sharing it with guests, please contact us on Etsy and we'll be happy to help.

Printable invitation

In the Print tab, choose Invite then PNG (digital) or PDF (print-ready). Standard 5×7 inches.

RSVP card

Under Print → RSVP Card, choose portrait (3.5×5) or landscape (5×3.5). QR code or contact info is baked in.

Details insert

Under Print → Details Insert, toggle sections on or off. You can now also enable a RSVP QR code directly on the details card — great for printed suites.

QR code note QR codes are generated online, so stay connected to the internet while adding or exporting any design that includes a QR code.
Print-at-home tip In your printer's settings, pick "Actual size" or "100%" — not "Fit to page" — so the card prints at the right dimensions.
